# Preliminary Sizing and General Arrangement - Naval Architecture

This project encompasses the initial design phases of a vessel, focusing on the **Spiral Design** methodology. Developed as part of the **Naval Architecture** course at **Instituto Superior Técnico (IST)**.

## Project Overview
The study involves the preliminary sizing of a Bulk Carrier based on mission requirements and statistical analysis of peer vessels. The project covers everything from main dimension definition to the final General Arrangement (GA).

## Key Design Phases
* **Statistical Dimensioning:** Definition of $L_{pp}$, $B$, $D$, and $T$ using regression analysis and deadweight requirements.
* **Hull Form & Hydrostatics:** Preliminary hull definition and calculation of hydrostatic curves.
* **General Arrangement (GA):** Detailed spatial organization including cargo holds, engine room location, tank distribution, and superstructure.
* **Stability & Trim:** Initial assessment of the center of gravity (KG) and metacentric height (GM) for various loading conditions.
* **Power Estimation:** Preliminary calculation of effective power and engine selection.

## Technical Skills Demonstrated
* **Iterative Design:** Understanding the "Design Spiral" and how changes in dimensions affect weight, stability, and cost.
* **Regulatory Compliance:** Application of international standards for tank arrangements and safety.
* **Technical Drawing:** Development of the General Arrangement plan.

## Tools Used
* **MS Excel:** For statistical regressions and weight estimation.
* **AutoCAD / Rhino:** For the General Arrangement and hull visualization.
